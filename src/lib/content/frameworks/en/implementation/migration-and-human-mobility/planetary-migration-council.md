## <a id="council-overview"></a>Beyond International Organizations: Planetary Coordination Architecture

### The Meta-Governance Breakthrough

Traditional international organizations operate through nation-state representation, creating governance that serves governmental interests rather than affected populations. The Planetary Migration Council breaks this pattern by centering **migrant voices**, **Indigenous sovereignty**, **youth authority**, and **community benefit** as the foundation for migration coordination.

**Polycentric Authority Distribution**: Rather than concentrating power in secretariats or powerful nations, the Council distributes authority across multiple centers with different types of expertise and constituency representation. No single actor can dominate decision-making while all participants maintain sovereignty over their core concerns.

**Golden Triangle Integration**: The Council demonstrates how **Treaty legal authority**, **Meta-Governance coordination**, **Indigenous territorial sovereignty**, and **Hearts currency validation** work together to create governance that serves rather than extracts from communities affected by migration policies.

### Constitutional Foundation Through Treaty Framework

**Legal Authority**: The Council operates under **Treaty for Our Only Home Pillar 1** institutional reforms with **binding international agreement** status rather than voluntary cooperation. This provides enforcement mechanisms and funding sources independent of nation-state political cycles.

**Universal Jurisdiction Integration**: Council decisions on **Migration Justice** receive enforcement through **Pillar 3** universal jurisdiction expansion, ensuring corporate accountability and ecocide prevention receive legal backing rather than remaining policy aspirations.

**Democratic Legitimacy**: All Council authority derives from **affected population representation** rather than governmental appointment, implementing **Pillar 2** participatory democracy innovations including **Global Citizens' Initiative** and **youth council authority**.

## <a id="decision-making"></a>Decision-Making Protocols: Consensus with Cultural Safeguards

### Modified Haudenosaunee Consensus: Traditional Wisdom for Planetary Governance

**Cultural Foundation**: Decision-making adapts **Haudenosaunee Confederacy** consensus protocols that have successfully governed across diverse nations for over 1,000 years, demonstrating that consensus can operate at large scales when properly structured.

**Consensus Threshold Requirements**:
- **Standard Decisions**: 70% agreement across all constituency groups with no group falling below 60% support
- **Emergency Decisions**: 60% agreement with **Traditional Knowledge Council** confirmation and **Youth Council** future impact approval
- **Long-Term Policy**: 75% agreement plus **Indigenous veto confirmation** and **seven-generation impact assessment** approval

**Indigenous Veto Protection**: Traditional communities exercise **absolute veto authority** over decisions affecting Indigenous territories, Traditional Knowledge, or cultural practices:
- **Territorial Sovereignty**: Automatic veto on policies affecting traditional lands and sacred sites
- **Cultural Protocol Protection**: Veto authority over migration policies conflicting with traditional governance and ceremonial requirements
- **Traditional Knowledge Sovereignty**: Absolute authority over appropriate sharing and application of Traditional Ecological Knowledge
- **Sacred Site Immunity**: Binding protection for culturally and spiritually significant areas from migration-related development

**Youth Future Impact Assessment**: Young people exercise **binding authority** over policies affecting their generations:
- **Seven-Generation Veto**: Authority to block decisions with harmful consequences for future generations
- **Climate Policy Authority**: Binding voice on climate displacement responses and adaptation strategies
- **Technology Governance**: Leadership authority over AI systems, digital infrastructure, and technological governance affecting their futures
- **Intergenerational Dialogue**: Required consultation between youth, adults, and elders on all major migration policies

- **Climate Displacement**: Sea level rise, extreme weather, or ecosystem collapse affecting 10,000+ people with Traditional Knowledge validation of environmental crisis
- **Conflict Migration**: War, persecution, or systematic violence creating mass displacement with human rights organization confirmation and Indigenous territorial impact assessment
- **Economic Crisis Displacement**: Financial collapse, resource extraction, or development displacement affecting multiple communities with community-controlled evidence gathering
- **Cultural Emergency**: Sacred site desecration, Traditional Knowledge appropriation, or cultural genocide threats affecting Indigenous communities

**24-Hour Response Coordination**: **Meta-Governance Framework** crisis coordination enabling rapid international action:
